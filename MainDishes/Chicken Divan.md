# Chicken Divan

**Source:** https://www.spendwithpennies.com/quick-simple-chicken-broccoli-divan/
**Author:** Holly Nilsson
**Servings:** 6
**Prep Time:** 15 minutes
**Cook Time:** 20 minutes
**Total Time:** 35 minutes

> A comforting casserole of tender chicken and broccoli in a rich, creamy sauce, topped with cheddar and a buttery breadcrumb crust.

---

## Ingredients

### Casserole
- 1 1/2 lbs broccoli florets, cut into bite-size pieces
- 3 cups cooked chicken, chopped
- 1 1/2 cups shredded cheddar cheese, divided
- 10.5 oz condensed cream of mushroom soup (1 can)
- 2/3 cup sour cream
- 1/2 cup milk
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1/2 tsp dry mustard powder
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/4 tsp seasoned salt

### Topping
- 3 tbsp Panko breadcrumbs
- 1 tbsp melted butter

---

## Instructions

1. **Preheat oven** to 400°F.
2. **Steam broccoli:** Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add broccoli florets and cook for 2–3 minutes until tender-crisp. Drain very well and set aside.
3. **Mix the sauce:** In a medium bowl, combine 1 cup of cheddar cheese, condensed soup, sour cream, milk, garlic powder, onion powder, dry mustard powder, black pepper, and seasoned salt. Stir until combined.
4. **Combine filling:** Stir the drained broccoli and chicken into the sauce mixture. Transfer everything to a 9x13-inch casserole dish. Top with the remaining 1/2 cup of cheddar cheese.
5. **Add breadcrumb topping:** In a small bowl, mix melted butter and Panko breadcrumbs together. Sprinkle evenly over the top of the casserole.
6. **Bake** for 18–20 minutes, or until the breadcrumbs are lightly browned and the mixture is bubbly and hot.
7. **Serve** over rice, egg noodles, or mashed potatoes, as desired.

---

## Notes

- **Chicken:** Use pre-cooked chicken — rotisserie chicken or poached chicken work great.
- **Broccoli:** Cook just until tender-crisp; it will soften more in the oven. Drain well to avoid a watery sauce. Frozen broccoli can be substituted but will be slightly softer.
- **Soup:** Any condensed "cream of" soup works, including cream of chicken.
- **Topping:** Crushed Ritz crackers or seasoned breadcrumbs can substitute for Panko.
- **Storage:** Leftovers keep in the fridge for up to 4 days and freeze well. Add a splash of milk when reheating.
